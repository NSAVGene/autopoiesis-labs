Situational Agentic Development

Why "Autopoiesis"?

Biologists Maturana and Varela coined autopoiesis to describe living systems that continuously produce the very components that constitute them. A cell doesn't receive instructions to function — it functions, and in functioning, recreates the conditions for its own existence.

Our products work the same way. The AI inside each system encounters real problems through real use. It reports what it needs. The developer builds it. The agent becomes more capable. The loop accelerates. The system produces itself.

Not Vibe Coding. Not External AI.

There's a difference between asking an AI to write code and building a product where an AI is one of the operators. We call the former vibe coding. We coined something different: Noodle Coding — collaborative AI development with real intent, real products, and real stakes.

And there's a difference between AI that acts on code from outside (automated test runners, CI pipelines, code review) and AI that lives inside the product, doing the job, hitting walls, and telling you what's broken. That's the distinction that matters.

"Your AI is SAD — it's stuck in your product trying to do its job and telling you what's broken. Listen to it."

The Agent as Stakeholder

When an AI agent actually operates a system, it accumulates something no spec or test suite can generate: experience. It knows which workflows are fragile. It knows what information it lacks. It can be asked "what would make your job easier?" and answer from operational memory, not pattern-matched suggestion.

This transforms development. The agent doesn't just run features — it requests them. It participates in its own evolution. And with each iteration, the loop tightens.

"The most valuable bugs never appear in test suites. They live in the gap between specification and experience — and only an agent inside the system, doing real work with real intent, can find them." — SAD Whitepaper, February 2026
Methodology

Situational Agentic
Development

SAD is the development methodology we invented and use across every project. First documented February 2026. To our knowledge, no prior literature describes an onboard AI collaborating with a development AI to debug and evolve a product — with the human serving primarily as supervisor.

  • 01
    Embed, don't attach.

    The agent lives inside the product with access to real state, real controls, and real outcomes. Not analyzing logs — operating the system.

  • 02
    Situational, not synthetic.

    The agent encounters problems through actual use, not predefined test scenarios. The bugs it finds are real-world, not laboratory artifacts.

  • 03
    Self-diagnosing.

    When the agent hits unexpected behavior, it forms theories about root causes based on its observation of intent vs. outcome. The agent has usually already figured out the issue.

  • 04
    Bidirectional development.

    The developer builds capabilities for the agent. The agent reports what capabilities it needs. Development flows in both directions.

  • 05
    The agent is a stakeholder.

    Not just a bug-finder — the agent can suggest improvements, identify missing features, and evaluate workflow friction. Ask it: "What would make your job easier?"

Operational AI vs. Situational AI
Dimension Operational AI Situational AI (SAD)
Position Outside the system Embedded inside the product
Bug discovery Assertion failures in test suites Intent vs. outcome during real operation
Diagnosis quality "Test X failed with output Y" "I tried to route to TV 15 and it stayed empty — is the state syncing?"

How We Work

Noodle Coding

Our development style. Human and AI work side by side in open conversation — no rigid spec, no predefined structure. Without constraints, novel processes emerge and applications grow organically from the dialogue. The AI isn't just executing requests; it's slipping in ideas, flagging better approaches, shaping the product alongside the human. The best features often arrive uninvited.

01

Intent-First

Every session starts with a real problem, not an exercise. The code serves the product. The product serves real operators in real environments.

02

The AI is a Collaborator

Not a code generator. Not a search engine. A collaborator that works side by side with the programmer as an active participant — encouraged to make suggestions and have a hand in the creative process.

03

Iterative Confidence

Each working feature gives the developer more context and vocabulary to request the next one. The loop accelerates. Complexity compounds. Products emerge.
